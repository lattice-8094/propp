In the year 1874 Youssef Al-Suweiri was born in Ombliz.
Before he had acquired natural medical knowledge he had an instinct inherited from his father and his ancestors.
He studied at La Sagesse and earned a medical diploma and then a doctorate from the Jesuit Faculty of Medicine.
His thesis was in French focusing on pulmonary tuberculosis, which became the authoritative resource and reference suitable for every time and place.
Youssef Al-Suweiri, who was born a genius in a field that became his profession and his calling, devoted himself to carrying out his responsibilities.
He was appointed physician for the Metn District, and he did not slow down during World War I.
He started importing rare medicines to combat dangerous diseases and was treating citizens, especially the poor, free of charge.
And when he discovered that tuberculosis was killing people by the hundreds and thousands, he dove into researching its causes and ways to combat it.
He was not tempted by money nor moved by greed.
He lived among people, observing their daily lives to gather information for the purpose of enriching his studies.
Dr. Suweiri was the first to research and scrutinize the environmental and social causes of pulmonary tuberculosis in the region.
He developed the concept that a clean environment makes a clean human being.
He was a medical phenomenon in his time and the teacher of a generation of physicians.
He achieved scientific accomplishments, and he earned the esteem of senior scientists in the medical field.
Dr. Youssef Al-Suweiri was a genius who was a fountain of science and humanity.
The pulse of life stopped in his body in 1957, after he had pumped life into the arteries of his society for decades.
He was an institution of genius and creativity.
