Israeli-Palestinian peace talks have ended in Egypt with a statement declaring the two sides are closer than ever to a final agreement.
The head of the Israeli delegation, Foreign Minister Shlomo Ben-Ami called the five days of talks constructive.
We have had the most truthful, constructive, profound negotiations ever to be held since this phase in the peace talks has started.
We feel that today we are closer than ever to the possibility of striking the final deal between us and the Palestinians.
Mr. Ben-Ami said there is possibility that Israeli Prime Minister Ehud Barak might meet with Palestinian leader Yasser Arafat next week.
The head of the Palestinian delegation, Ahmed Kurai says if opposition candidate Ariel Sharon comes to power in Israel's election next month, the Palestinians are ready to negotiate with him.
