Iran today held elections for the Leadership Experts Council, in addition to municipal councils and complementary elections for the Islamic Consultative Assembly.
Numerous lists are competing in these elections.
Competition was greater for the municipal councils which are close to the lives of citizens and the services these councils provide to solve these citizens ' problems such as unemployment and inflation.
The Leadership Experts Council bears responsibility for supervising the work of the supreme leader or guide of the political regime in addition to elections if there is n't one.
Candidates for the Leadership Experts Council are required to be highly knowledgeable in Islamic religious sciences whereas candidates to municipal councils do not have to be.
The conservatives were divided into three lists in the municipal elections. While the reformists lined up in one list in the hope of winning these elections which are considered an indicator for upcoming elections, whether presidential or Consultative Assembly elections.
Of the three conservative lists, the first backed Iranian President Ahmedinejad. While the second backed Tehran's mayor and the former presidential candidate, Mohammed Baqer Qalibaf.
The Chairman of the Consultative Assembly, Haddad Adel, backed the third list called the Reformist Conservatives.
While the conservatives monopolized the Leadership Experts Councils, competition for the municipal councils was abundant.
Results for these council elections indicate the acceptability of the presidency of President Ahmedinejad.
They also have an effect on the upcoming parliamentary and presidential elections.
From a voting center, Mohammed Salih Sadqi, Kuwait State Television, Tehran.
