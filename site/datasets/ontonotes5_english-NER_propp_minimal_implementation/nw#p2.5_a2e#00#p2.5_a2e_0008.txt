Mitsutaka Uchikoshi, a 35 year old Japanese man, went missing on the seventh of last October, according to news agencies and the British Broadcasting Company " B. B. C., " after his attempt to descend Mount Rokko in western Japan.
And the " B. B. C. " mentioned yesterday-LRB-Thursday-RRB-that when Uchikoshi was found, he had no pulse, his vital organs had shut down completely, and his temperature had dropped to 22 degrees.
The medical team that found Uchikoshi said, according to the agency " U. B. A., " that they were stunned when they discovered he was still alive.
" I fell down in a green area, and at first I felt the warmth of the sun.
Then, I fell asleep, " said Uchikoshi, who thinks that his foot slipped and he fainted when he attempted to descend the mountain.
" That is all I remember, " he added.
A rescue team found Uchikoshi on the thirty-first of last October.
" At first, Uchikoshi's temperature dropped below the normal level, a state similar to hibernation, " said Dr. Shinichi Sato, adding that " His brain was protected and did not suffer any damage and is now 100 % recovered. "
Uchikoshi was released from the hospital and returned home last Tuesday after it was confirmed he had fully recovered.
