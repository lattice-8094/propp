The Israeli Prime Minister Ehud Barak is due to meet with Egypt's President Mubarak in Cairo on Thursday.
The encounter is being seen as an effort by Barak to drum up regional support for a Middle East peace agreement based on President Clinton's proposals.
They require Israel to relinquish sovereignty over most of Arab East Jerusalem and the Palestinians to scale back their demand for a return of refugees, but Palestinian chief negotiator Sayeb Erekat says more details are required before both sides can strike a deal.
